Fact 1: Honey Never Spoils

Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are thousands of years old—and still perfectly edible.

Why doesn't honey go bad?

It comes down to its chemistry. Honey is very low in moisture and highly acidic. This creates an incredibly hostile environment for bacteria and microorganisms, meaning they cannot survive or multiply in it to cause spoilage.

Fact 2: Sharks Are Older Than Trees

The earliest evidence of shark scales dates back around 450 million years. Meanwhile, the earliest species that we would classify as a tree (like the Archaeopteris) evolved around 350 to 370 million years ago.

How did sharks survive multiple mass extinctions if so many other species died out?

Sharks possess incredible biological flexibility. They have diverse diets, can live at various ocean depths, and their cartilaginous skeletons require less energy to maintain than bony skeletons, helping them adapt when global environments shifted drastically.

Fact 3: Venus Spins Backwards

In our solar system, nearly all planets rotate on their axes in a counter-clockwise direction (prograde). Venus is one of the exceptions, rotating clockwise (retrograde).

What caused Venus to start spinning in reverse?

Astronomers aren't entirely certain, but the leading theory is that billions of years ago,Venus was struck by a massive celestial body. This colossal impact was likely powerful enough to completely alter the planet's rotational direction.
